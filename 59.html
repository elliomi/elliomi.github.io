    Apparently during the war years Leo wanted to join the navy but nanna did not want him to. Then eventually he went to join but they could not accept him because he was colour blind. His youngest brother, Kevin, is also colour blind. He worked as an electrician during the war doing maintenance on the hospital ships in port.

    Beryl worked at the milk bar at the picture theatre on Parramatta Road, Granville with Pat and that is where Pat introduced Beryl to Leo, one night at the shop. It is rare in this family for the 11 to have met their future spouses through any other means than each other!

    Leo and Beryl were married at St Patrick's Church Parramatta. They initially lived in Wigram Street. Harris Park. The\ eventually settled at Pendle Hill where they raised their two girls Julie and Tracey.

    Leo too was an electrician and went into business with Len at one stage with the company name Elliott Brothers. Dad also worked with Leo at Wright Brothers and Bartholomew's.

    Leo used to always call in to visit everyone whenever he could. He would always call in to Aunty Madges and in later years when uncle Albert died he would always visit Val until he himself got a form of dementia.

    Both Uncle Leo and Aunty Pat had holiday places at Budgiwoi in the central coast and their houses used to back onto the reserve to the lake and they water-skied. Uncle Albert also had a holiday caravan there.

    Marie Melinz (nee Wright) recalls that when the Elliott cousins came to visit they would always chip in and help with the chores. She recalls Uncle Leo saying he will wash up and then after the Elliott family had left they would find the dirty pots and pans under the table (Uncle Leo was a typical "Wright" prankster).
